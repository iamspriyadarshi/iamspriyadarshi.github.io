Humans are composed of 50 to 70 trillion cells. These cells form a huge range of tissues all specialized and completely different from each other. You must have thought that why we don't have an eye in place of a nose and nose in place of eyes? We all know that every cell in our body is derived from just one cell called a zygote. A zygote is formed when a sperm fuses with an egg. The zygote then divides into two and those two divides again and again to form the full human body. As the cells divide, they differentiate. Differentiation is a vital process in the formation of any multicellular organism.

{% include image.html url="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/iamspriyadarshi/iamspriyadarshi.github.io/master/images/Differentiation.jpg" description="Mechanisms of cellular differentiation." %}

If we look at the cell from the liver and the cell from the brain, both are completely different. Even though both types of cells started with the same genetic material with just one blueprint, both cells are completely different. This might be because the cells are using the same information in different ways or as the cells become more specialized, they rearrange their genetic material, possibly losing genes they are not going to use.

You might even be thinking why twins are not completely identical even though they share the same genetic material. An organism not only develop according to their genetic code but it also develops according to nature surrounding it. The DNA in our cell is not completely pure. A small chemical group can be added to a specific region of DNA. These changes can alter the function of cells and thus the nature of cells.

You will be shocked to know that 98 percent of DNA in a human cell is junk. It doesn't code for any protein. For 50 letters of DNA, one letter is junk. Then, why it is available? We can understand this with the help of an example. Suppose we visit a car manufacturing party. We see there that for every two people who were building a car, there were another 98 who were doing all the things that keep the business moving. That's exactly in the case of DNA. One another shocking fact is that human contains the same amount of protein-coding gene as that of some simple microscopic worm. Then, why a human is more complex than a worm? It's because of junk DNA. The amount of junk DNA in humans is more than that of a worm. The junk DNA performs multiple functions. It prevents DNA from damaging. It's also an indicator of aging. As we age, these regions decrease in size. It also restricts gene expression. It doesn't produce protein but it forms a factory in the cell, helping to produce protein. It also forms molecules that help in transporting the raw material for protein production to the factory site.

Although junk DNA doesn't form protein, the mutation in this DNA can cause genetic disease also. We don't have the appropriate technology to know more about junk DNA. Organism development is not a result of just protein-coding DNA but also of junk DNA that assists the protein-coding gene.
